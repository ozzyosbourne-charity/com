Remembering The Prince of Darkness

Ozzy Osbourne, the legendary Black Sabbath frontman and solo artist, passed away on July 22, 2025 at the age of 76. While known worldwide as the "Prince of Darkness" for his groundbreaking heavy metal music, Ozzy was also a devoted philanthropist who supported numerous charitable causes throughout his life.

This memorial fund continues Ozzy's legacy of generosity, supporting the causes he cared about most: medical research, children's health, and helping those in need.

The Final Charity Concert

July 15, 2025 - Villa Park, Birmingham

One week before his passing, Ozzy performed his final concert - a spectacular charity event that brought together generations of musicians and fans. The "Ozzy and Friends" concert featured special appearances by Metallica, Foo Fighters, Post Malone, and members of Black Sabbath.

The emotional evening saw Ozzy perform many of his classic hits despite his health challenges, demonstrating his incredible dedication to both his art and his charitable mission.

All proceeds from this historic concert are being donated to three organizations close to Ozzy's heart:

  • Cure Parkinson's - Supporting research into treatments for the condition Ozzy battled in his later years
  • Birmingham Children's Hospital - Helping sick children in his hometown
  • Acorn Children's Hospice - Providing care for children with life-limiting conditions
